Arborvitae

Arborvitae (Thuja) is a type of evergreen tree or shrub that is often associated with various symbolic meanings. The term "arborvitae" is Latin for "tree of life," and it has been used in different cultures and contexts to symbolize various ideas and concepts. Some common symbolic meanings associated with arborvitae include:

“everlasting friendship”

Longevity

The name "tree of life" suggests longevity and immortality, and arborvitae can symbolize a long and healthy life.

Endurance

Due to its evergreen nature, arborvitae can symbolize endurance, resilience, and the ability to withstand challenges and harsh conditions.

Vitality

Arborvitae's green foliage symbolizes life, growth, and vitality. It can represent the renewal of life and hope.

Protection

In some cultures, arborvitae is believed to provide protection and ward off negative energies or spirits when planted near homes or temples.

Friendship + Peace

In some Native American cultures, arborvitae is associated with friendship and peace. The tree's branches were sometimes used in making peace pipes.

Healing + Medicinal

Arborvitae has been used in traditional medicine by some indigenous cultures, and it can symbolize healing and wellness.

Spiritual + Religious

In some spiritual and religious traditions, arborvitae is considered a sacred tree and can symbolize spiritual growth, connection, or enlightenment.
