Calla Lily

The calla lily (Zantedeschia spp.) is a beautiful and elegant flower known for its distinctive trumpet-shaped bloom. Its symbolic meanings can vary depending on the culture and context, but some common associations with calla lilies include:

“pure innocence”

Purity + Innocence

Calla lilies are often associated with purity, innocence, and youth. They are frequently used in weddings and other celebrations to symbolize the pure and unblemished nature of love and new beginnings.

Beauty

The striking and graceful appearance of calla lilies makes them a symbol of beauty and refinement. They are admired for their elegance and sophistication.

Marriage + Fidelity

In the language of flowers, calla lilies can represent marital bliss and fidelity, making them a popular choice for wedding bouquets and decorations.

Resurrection + Rebirth

In some cultures, calla lilies symbolize resurrection and renewal. This symbolism is derived from the flower's ability to bloom anew after a period of dormancy.

Sympathy + Mourning

In other contexts, calla lilies are used as symbols of sympathy and condolences, particularly in funeral arrangements. They convey a sense of hope and the continuation of life beyond death.

Eroticism + Desire

The sensuous shape of the calla lily's flower has led to associations with sexuality and desire in some cultures. It is sometimes considered a symbol of passion.
